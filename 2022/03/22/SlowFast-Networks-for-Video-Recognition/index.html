SlowFast Networks for Video Recognition

Abstract

Our model involves
(i) Slow pathway, operating at low frame rate, to capture spatial semantics
(ii) Fast pathway, operating at high frame rate, to capture motion at fine temporal resolution

The Fast pathway can be made veer ligthweight by reducing its channel capacity, yet can learn useful temporal inforamtion for video recognition.

Our models achieve strong performance for both action classification and detection in video, and large improvements are pin-pointed as contributions by our SlowFast concept.

1. Introduction

Motion is spatiotemporal counterpart of orientation, but all spatiotemporal orientations are not equally likely.

Slow motions are likely than fast motion(indeed most of the world we see id at rest at a given moment) and this has been exploited in Bayesian accounts of how humans perceive motion stimuli[58].
For example, if we see a moving edge in isolation, we perceive it could also have an arbitary component of movement tangential to itself (the aperture problem in optical flow). This percept is rantional if the prior favors slow movements.

for concreteness

For example, waving hands do not change their identity as “hands” over the span of the waving action, and a person is always in the “person” category even though he/she can transit from walking to running. So the recognitaion of the categorial sematics (as well as their colors, textures, lighting etc.) can be refreshed relatively slowly. On the other hand, the motion being performed can evolve much faster than their subject identities, such as clapping, waving shaking, walking, or jumping.
It can be desired to use fast refreshing frames (high temporal resolution) to effectively model the potentially fast changing motion.

Based on this intuition,
we present a two-pathway SlowFast model for video recognitation.
One pathway is designed to capture sematic information that can be given by images or a few sparse frames, and it operates at low frame rates and slow refreshing speed.
In constrast,
the other pathway is responsible for capturing rapidly changing motion, by operating at fast refresh speed and high temporal resolution.

This is because this pathway is designed to have fewer channels and weaker ability to process spatial information, while such information can be provided by the first pathway in a less redundant manner.

The tow pathways are fused by lateral connections.

The Fast pathway, due to its lightweight nature, does not need to perform any temporal pooling – it can operate on high frame rates for all intermediate layers and maintain temporal rate, the Slow pathway can be more focused on the spatil domanin and semantics.

There is another well known architecture for video recognition which has a two-stream design[44], but provides conceptually different perspectives. The Two-Stream method has not explored the potential of different temporal speeds, a key concept in our method.

Our method is partially inspired by biological studies on the retinal ganglion cell in the primate visual system[27,37,8,14,51] and premature.

Our framework is analogous in that:

  1. our model has two pathways sparately working at low and high temporal resolutions;
  2. our Fast pathway is designed to capture fast changing motion but fewer spatial detials, analogous to M-cells;
  3. our Fast pathway is lightweight, similar to the small ratio of M-cells.
    We hope these relations will inspire more computer vision models for video recognitation.

We evaluate our method on the Kinetics-400[30], Kinetics-600[3], Charades[43] and AVA[20] datasets.

SlowFast networks set a new state-of-the-art on all datasets with significant gains to previous systems in the literature.

2. Related Work

Spatiotemporal filtering. Actions can be formulated as spatiotemporal objects and captured by oriented filtering in spacetime, as done by HOG3D and cuboids[10]. 3D ConvNets[48,49,5] extend 2D image models[32,45,47,24] to the spatiotemporal domain, handing both spatial and temporal dimension similarly. There are also related methods focusing on long-term filering and pooling using temporal strides[52,13,55,62], as well as decomposing the convolutions into separate 2D spatial and 1D temporal fliters[12,50,61,39].
Beyond spatiotemporal filtering or their separable versions, our work pursuits a more thorough separation of modeling expertise by using two different temporal speed.

Optical flow for video recognition There is a classical branch of research focusing on hand-crafted spatiotemporal features based on optical flow. There methods, including histograms of flow[33], motion boundary histograms[6], and trajectories[53], had shown competitive performance for action recognition before the prevalence of deep learning.

In the context of deep neural networks, the two-stream method[44],exploits optical flow by viewing it as another input modaity. This method has been a foundation of many competitive results in the literature[12,13,55].
However, it is methodologically unsatisfactory given than optical flow is a hand-designed representation, and two-stream methods are often not learned end-to-end jointly with flow.

Interestingly, as mentioned in Sec. 1, evidence suggests that ~15-20% of the retinal cells in the primate vidual system are M-cells (that are sensitive to fast motion but not color or spatial detail).

The low channel capacity can also be interpreted as a weaker ability of representing spatial treatment on the spatial dimensin, so its spatial modeling capacity should be lower than the Slow pathway because of fewer channels.

Motivated by this interpretation, we also explore different ways of weakening spatial capacity in the Fast pathway, including reducing input spatial resolution and removing color information.
As we will show by experiments, these versions can all give good accuracy, suggesting that a lightweight Fast pathway with less spatial capacity can be made benefitical.

3.3. Lateral connections

The information of the two pathways is fused, so one pathway is not unaware of the representation learned by the other pathway.
We inplement this by lateral connections, which have been used to fuse optical flow-based, two-stream networks[12,13]. In image object detection, lateral connections[35] are popular technique for merging different levels of spatial resolution and semantics.

Unlike typical C3D/I3D models, we use non-degernerate temporal convoluations (temporal kernel size > 1, underlined in Table 1) only in res4 and res5; all filters from conv1 to res3 are essentially 2D convoluation kernels in this pathway.
This is motivated by our experimental observation that using temporal convoluations in earlier layers degrades accuracy.
We argue that this is because whenobjects move fast and the temporal stride is large, there is little correlation within a temporal receptive field unless the spatial receptive field is large enough.

5.Experiments: AVA Action Detection

Dataset The AVA dataset focuses on spatiotemporal localization of human actions. The data set is taken from 437 movies. Spatiotemporal labels are provided for one frame per second, with every person annotated with a bounding box and (possibly multiple) actions.

The performance metrics is mean Average Precision(mAP) over 60 classes, using a frame-level IoU thershold of 0.5.

Detection architecture. Our detector is similar fo Fast R-CNN with minimal modification adapted for video. We use the SlowFast network or its variants as the backbone.

6. Conclusion

The time axis is a special dimension. This paper has investigated an architecture design that contrasts the speed along this axis. It achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for video action classification and detection. We hope that this SlowFast concept will foster further research in video recognition.
